1.1 The Brutalist Era

12:45 / 42:00

Synthesis of Form and Function

Brutalism, characterized by its use of raw concrete and heavy, monumental forms, emerged in the mid-20th century as a reaction against the lightness of early modern architecture. In this lesson, we explore the ethical underpinnings of "Béton brut" and how architects like Le Corbusier and Alison and Peter Smithson redefined social housing through massive scale and uncompromising materiality.

We will examine the controversy surrounding these structures—their perception as "cold" versus their intent as honest, utilitarian spaces for the post-war public.

lightbulb

Key Concept

The term "Brutalism" isn't about being 'brutal', but from 'Béton brut'—French for raw concrete.

history_edu

Historical Context

Post-WWII Europe required rapid, cost-effective reconstruction using readily available materials.
